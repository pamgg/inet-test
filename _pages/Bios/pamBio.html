Pamela Gionco (Bio)


I was born in 1982, the same year that the Times Magazine selected Personal Computer as "Person of the Year".
In my family, it is said that I learned to type my name on the keyboard before I learned how to hand-write it. I still remember an amber monochrome CRT monitor where I used to play old video games.
I started using Windows when you should type the command "win" in DOS, a little time before it became an auto launch program that invade and conquer all computers an Operating System.
In 2000, the 2YK found me finishing high school... but the world didn't end then.

In spite all that, I studied Humanities.
To be more specific, I have a degree in Art History and Theory and I'm assistant professor and researcher on Film and Media Studies, both in the Philosophy and Literature School of the Universidad de Buenos Aires.
I also took postgraduate courses in Library and Information Science.
I work as a technical assistant in Digitization Project Management in a national cultural institution.

So, here I am/we are, trying to deal with the idea (and the making) of Digital Humanities.
